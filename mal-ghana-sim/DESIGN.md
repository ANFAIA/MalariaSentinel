## Problem Statement
Malaria surveillance is reactive: interventions launch only after clinical cases appear, when an outbreak is already expanding. MalariaSentinel's goal (per `casablanca/README.md`) is a predictive AI system that flags the micro-zones that will become infection hotspots *before* cases spike.

This design covers the first concrete building block: a **mosquito spread simulation** for Ghana that (a) produces a habitat-suitability / risk map of where *Anopheles* mosquitoes could reside and spread, and (b) serves as the **synthetic-data teacher for a U-Net** that learns the spread transition operator — a fast, deployable surrogate that predicts expansion from any seed point. The demo runs on Ghana; the architecture generalizes to the other datasets on hand (Colombia, Nigeria, REACT / Côte d'Ivoire).

## What Makes This Cool
A mechanistic simulator grounded in **real environmental rasters + 24 larval breeding-site records** acts as a "physics engine" that generates spatio-temporal training frames; a **U-Net learns the transition operator** and becomes a fast spread predictor. This is the scientific-ML *teacher–surrogate* pattern (cf. neural PDE surrogates) applied to disease ecology. The rare, valuable piece is that the occurrence data is **larval** — it directly labels breeding-site suitability, the correct ground truth for the suitability layer that drives the whole pipeline.

## Constraints
- **Demo scope:** Ghana, AOI = the existing SRTM DEM bbox `lon[-2.97, 0.79] lat[4.69, 9.79]` (~417 km × 566 km, Northern Ghana / Tolon region). The 24 occurrence sites span Ghana's ecozones; most lie in-AOI. **Confirm the exact in-AOI count from `occurrence.txt` at M2** — the AUC criterion assumes ≥15 in-AOI sites. If fewer fall inside, extend the DEM bbox via `srtm_all_locations.py:73` (refetch SRTMGL1 over a larger bbox) before proceeding.
- **Local data, no cloud *compute* dependency** (Approach A): data fetched to disk. Free API **auth** is required and accepted: OpenTopography API key for SRTM (already used in `srtm_all_locations.py`), NASA Earthdata login for MODIS (AppEEARS). No Google Earth Engine for v1.
- **Compute:** single workstation. Simulator runs on CPU (numpy/scipy on a ~417×566 grid is trivial). **U-Net training requires a GPU** (CPU is infeasible for ~5–8M params × tens of thousands of patches × 20–50 epochs); inference is CPU-feasible.
- **Reuse existing stack:** `mapa_anopheles/` (uv, pandas, cartopy, geopandas, rasterio, contextily, scipy) + the `data/ghana_idit/occurrence.txt` loader pattern from `ghana_map.py`.
- **Existing assets:** SRTMGL1 30 m DEM (168 MB) at `MalariaStudies/srtm_maps/ghana_idit/`; 1,008 larval *Anopheles gambiae* s.l. records (24 sites) at `MalariaStudies/data/ghana_idit/occurrence.txt`; working DEM download/plot script at `srtm_all_locations.py`.
- **No temporal incidence data yet** — spread dynamics are simulator-internal and hypothesis-generating. The design includes a hook to ingest malaria-incidence field data later for real grounding.

## Premises (agreed during office hours)
1. **Grain of model:** the objective is a habitat-suitability + spread model, not a full agent-based simulation tracking individual mosquitoes. A grid-level **reaction–diffusion** model is the right mechanistic grain — physically interpretable, cheap, and produces the spreading fronts the U-Net should learn.
2. **Layer roles:** elevation (SRTM) is a *secondary* driver; **water, rainfall, temperature, NDVI** are the primary *Anopheles* habitat predictors (per `CoreHypothesis/` literature). **Population is a human-exposure layer, not a mosquito-habitat layer** — deferred to v2 (human-risk variant), not part of v1.
3. **Sim-to-real caveat:** the U-Net learns the *simulator's* rules, not reality. The 24 larval sites validate the **suitability** layer; the **spread dynamics** are unvalidated until temporal incidence data is ingested. This is stated openly, not hidden.
4. **Static-env transition operator (v1):** all v1 env layers are **long-term climatological normals** (WorldClim BIO normals, CHIRPSclim, MODIS multi-year annual mean). There is no temporal variation across rollouts; dynamics variation comes **only from param randomization**. The U-Net therefore learns a *static-env* transition operator. Temporal climate sequences (per-season or daily) are a v2.
5. **Working resolution: 1 km grid in EPSG:32630 (UTM 30N)** — metres, not degrees. 30 m / 100 m / 5 km layers reprojected+resampled to this 1000 m grid. 500 m / 250 m are quality knobs for later.
6. **U-Net intent: both** — deployable product *and* fast-sweep surrogate. The simulator stays the source of truth; the U-Net is the fast rollout engine.

## Approaches Considered
### Approach A: Minimal-viable demo pipeline *(chosen)*
Weighted-overlay suitability → simple numpy reaction–diffusion sim → vanilla U-Net transition surrogate → matplotlib/cartopy risk-expansion map. All local data, reusing `mapa_anopheles`. Effort S–M, risk low. Ships the full teacher→surrogate loop on Ghana in days. *(No cloud compute; free API auth accepted.)*

### Approach B: Rigor-first
MaxEnt suitability on the 24 larval presences; literature-parameterized simulator with a calibration hook for future incidence data; regularized U-Net. Scientifically defensible and real-validation-ready, but weeks to a visible demo. MaxEnt on 24 points is thin (needs spatial thinning / CV).

### Approach C: Cloud-native (Google Earth Engine)
Pull/clip all env layers via GEE instead of local downloads; same modeling as A. Best when expanding to Colombia/Nigeria/REACT, but adds a cloud compute dependency + GEE auth for the demo.

## Recommended Approach
**Approach A** — it's the fastest path to a working teacher→surrogate loop on data already in hand, and it reuses the existing stack directly. B's rigor (MaxEnt + literature params) and C's EE sourcing become deliberate v1.5/v2 upgrades once the demo loop is proven. Detailed architecture follows.

### Stage 1 — Data ingestion
- Define AOI bbox from the SRTM DEM (`srtm_maps/ghana_idit/SRTMGL1_-2.97_0.79_4.69_9.79.tif`).
- **CRS & grid:** reproject everything to **EPSG:32630 (UTM 30N) at 1000 m** (metres, not degrees). SRTM is the reference grid; all other layers `reproject_match` to the SRTM-aggregated 1 km grid via `rioxarray`.
- Per-layer **nodata policy** (table above): water → 0 (land); temp/elev/NDVI/rainfall → AOI-mean fill; MODIS cloud/QC pixels masked out (VIQC bits) before averaging.
- Output: a single `(C, H, W)` `np.float32` array (`C` = elevation, water_frac, rainfall, temp, ndvi) + `affine`/`crs` sidecar, cached as `.npz` + a GeoTIFF for viz.
- Reuse the raster-read helper pattern from `srtm_all_locations.py:89` (`read_raster`).

### Stage 2 — Suitability model (carrying-capacity field)
- Normalize each layer to 0–1. **Temperature suitability curve** (citing Mordecai et al. 2013, *An. gambiae* thermal response): v1 approximation `s(T) = max(0, 1 − ((T − 25) / 8)² )` — parabolic, optimum 25 °C, zero outside ~17–33 °C. Applied to BIO1.
- **Weighted linear overlay** (Multi-Criteria Evaluation), v1 weights (literature-justified, explicitly tunable in `config.py`):
  - water fraction / proximity: **0.35**
  - rainfall: **0.20**
  - temperature suitability `s(T)`: **0.20**
  - NDVI: **0.15**
  - elevation: **0.10**
- Carrying capacity `K(cell) = suitability(cell) × K_max`, with `K_max = 1000` (mosquitoes/cell, v1). K is **per-cell** (heterogeneous).
- **Validation:** presence-only **AUC** of the suitability field against the in-AOI larval sites, ~10 000 random background points. Report **bootstrap 95% CI** (1000 resamples). **Criterion: lower 95% bound > 0.65** (a single point estimate with ≤24 presences has CI ±0.1–0.15, so the lower bound — not the point estimate — is the bar).

### Stage 3 — Mechanistic simulator (reaction–diffusion)
State `D[t]` = mosquito density per 1 km cell (0..K(cell)). One step ≈ 1 day (matches ~1 km/step dispersal on a 1 km grid). Per step:
1. **Growth** (logistic toward K, only where `water_frac > water_thresh=0.1`): `D += r(T)·D·(1 − D/K)·water_mask`, where `r(T) = r_max · max(0, 1 − ((T−25)/8)²)` — **reproduction is temperature-gated** (parabolic thermal response, optimum 25 °C, zero outside ~17–33 °C; Mordecai et al. 2013 gonotrophic-cycle / larval-development response), not a constant. This is the key temperature→reproducibility link: mosquitoes reproduce fastest near 25 °C and stop reproducing outside the viable range, independent of the mortality term below.
2. **Dispersal** (4-neighbor diffusion via `scipy.ndimage.laplace`, kernel `[[0,1,0],[1,-4,1],[0,1,0]]`): `D += μ·laplace(D)`. **μ=0.2**, under the explicit-Euler stability bound μ ≤ 2/8 = 0.25 for this kernel (verified; no implicit solver needed for v1).
3. **Mortality** (temperature-dependent): `D *= (1 − m(T))`, with `m(T) = 0.05 + 0.2·max(0, ((T − 25)/8)²)` — ~0.05/day at 25 °C, rising parabolically outside the viable range (mirrors the `s(T)` curve). Cited to Mordecai et al. 2013 as the v1 approximation; coefficients tunable.

**v1 parameters** (`config.py`, all explicitly tunable, sourced-to-literature later):
| param | v1 value | meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `r_max` | 0.30 /step | max logistic growth rate; effective `r(T) = r_max·max(0,1−((T−25)/8)²)` (temperature-gated reproduction) |
| `μ` | 0.20 | diffusion coefficient (≤0.25 stable, 4-neighbor) |
| `K_max` | 1000 | carrying capacity at max suitability |
| `water_thresh` | 0.10 | water_frac above which breeding occurs |
| `m(T)` | `0.05 + 0.2·max(0,((T−25)/8)²)` | per-step mortality |
| `N` | 60 steps (~2 months, ~1 day/step) | rollout length (one transmission-season segment) |
| `T` | 5 steps (weekly) | snapshot interval → 12 transition pairs/rollout |

Seed: `D_0 = 0.1·K(cell)` at seeded cells (occurrence sites **or** a random single-seed point), 0 elsewhere. Snapshots saved every T as `.npz` + GeoTIFF. This is a **Fisher-KPP-style reaction–diffusion with heterogeneous K** — well-understood, produces clean expanding fronts, exactly the dynamics a U-Net transition operator can learn.

### Stage 4 — Training-set assembly
- Generate **≥100 rollouts** with **randomized parameters** sampled uniformly:
  | param | low | high |
  |---|---|---|
  | `r_max` | 0.15 | 0.45 |
  | `μ` | 0.10 | 0.22 |
  | `K_max` | 500 | 1500 |
  | `water_thresh` | 0.05 | 0.20 |
  …and **randomized seeds** (single-point seeds + real-site seeds) so the U-Net generalizes across dynamics, not one param set.
- From each rollout's snapshots, build **12 transition pairs per rollout** `(D_0→D_T, D_T→D_{2T}, …, D_{N−T}→D_N)`. **Tile** the ~417×566 image into **128×128 patches, stride 64 (50% overlap), edges reflect-padded** to a divisible size → ~50 000–75 000 patches total (≥100 rollouts × 12 pairs × ~50 patches/frame).
- **Split by AOI sub-region:** west ~80% → train, east ~20% → val (spatially disjoint, no shared env/sites — not a patch-random split).
- Normalize density: `D_norm = D / K_max` (so the U-Net sees [0,1]).
- **Augmentation (regularization):** random flips, 90° rotations, and Gaussian noise on `D_t` (σ = 0.01 in normalized [0,1] units).

### Stage 5 — U-Net transition surrogate
- **Input channels (6):** `D_t_norm, water_frac, rainfall, temp_suitability s(T), NDVI, elevation`.
- **Target:** `D_{t+T}_norm` (1 channel).
- **Architecture:** standard U-Net, 4 down/up blocks, channel widths 32→64→128→256, **BatchNorm + ReLU**, **skip connections via concatenation**, bilinear upsampling. ~5–8M params. Input patch 128×128.
- **Loss:** `L = MSE + 0.5·(1 − soft_Dice)`, where **soft Dice** uses a differentiable relaxation `σ((D_norm − 0.1·suitability)/τ)` (τ=0.05) as the "presence" probability — the hard threshold `D_norm > 0.1·suitability` is used **only for the eval metric**, never in the loss (a hard `>` has zero gradient and would silently reduce training to MSE-only).
- **Training:** PyTorch, Adam (lr 1e-3), 20–50 epochs, batch 16–32. **GPU required.**

### Stage 6 — Inference & output
- Given a seed point (or the 24 sites), set `D_0`, then either run the simulator (ground truth) **or** roll the trained U-Net forward `k·T` steps to get the predicted expansion field.
- **Output map (SRTM-style):** continuous risk-expansion raster (0–1) over the AOI, rendered with `matplotlib`/`cartopy` (hillshade from SRTM as backdrop, risk as a translucent heat layer, occurrence sites + seed overlaid) — reusing the viz style from `srtm_all_locations.py:100` (`plot_dem`).
- Side-by-side: simulator rollout vs U-Net rollout (shows the surrogate tracks the teacher).

### Validation plan
- **Grounded now:** suitability-layer **AUC + bootstrap 95% CI** vs in-AOI larval sites (Stage 2); criterion = lower 95% bound > 0.65. U-Net-vs-simulator agreement on held-out (east sub-region) rollouts — **MSE** and **front-overlap Dice** on the hard mask `D_norm > 0.1·suitability` (local K, D normalized by `K_max`); criterion Dice > 0.6. This validates the *surrogate*, not reality.
- **Honest gap:** spread dynamics are **not** validated against reality (v1 env is static climatological normals; dynamics vary only by param randomization). Documented as a known limitation in the README.
- **Later (v1.5):** a calibration hook (Stage 3 parameter sweep / ensemble) that assimilates malaria-incidence field data when available — the real-world grounding the user described ("use it in the real world with field analysis from malaria incidences").

## Open Questions
- **MaxEnt vs weighted overlay (v1.5):** with only ~24 presences, weighted overlay is the pragmatic v1; MaxEnt (via `elapid`) with spatial thinning + leave-one-site-out CV is the rigor upgrade once the loop works.
- **Google Earth Engine (v2):** switch ingestion to GEE when expanding to Colombia/Nigeria/REACT (Approach C).
- **Incidence assimilation (v1.5):** what format will the Ghana malaria-incidence field data arrive in (DHMIS weekly counts by district? point-referenced case data?) — the calibration hook's shape depends on it.
- **U-Net architecture (v2):** vanilla U-Net is fine for single-step `t→t+T`; if full temporal *sequences* matter, upgrade to **ConvLSTM**; if the PDE-surrogate framing dominates, a **Fourier Neural Operator** is the literature standard.
- **Temporal climate (v2):** v1 uses static climatological normals; per-season or daily climate sequences would let the U-Net learn true temporal dynamics (currently only param randomization varies).
- **Parameter grounding:** v1 params are guesses; sourcing defensible *An. gambiae* values from `CoreHypothesis/` PDFs is a later task (Approach B territory).
