# Spatial modeling of the population dynamics of Anopheles mosquitoes in Madagascar

**Authors:** Rakotoarison et al.
**Journal:** HAL (preprint) | **Year:** 2025 | **DOI:** hal-05382643v1
**File:** papers/anopheles-dynamics/rakotoarison-et-al-2025-anopheles-madagascar.md

## Abstract

This paper develops a spatiotemporal dynamics model for four *Anopheles* species in Madagascar, explicitly integrating the agricultural calendar to adjust environmental carrying capacity. The model demonstrates robustness across sites with diverse climates and accurately reproduces interannual population dynamics. The agricultural calendar integration provides a novel mechanism for capturing human-modified habitat effects on vector populations.

## Methods

- Multi-species spatiotemporal population dynamics model for four Anopheles species
- Agricultural calendar integration to modulate environmental carrying capacity
- Environmental drivers: temperature, precipitation, humidity from gridded climate data
- Validation against longitudinal mosquito collection data from multiple Madagascar sites
- Bayesian parameter estimation for species-specific life history traits
- Cross-site validation across diverse climatic zones

## Key Results

- Agricultural calendar significantly affects mosquito population dynamics through habitat modification
- Model accurately reproduces interannual variability across sites with diverse climates
- Multi-species approach captures community-level dynamics missed by single-species models
- Seasonal patterns driven by interaction between climate and agricultural cycles
- Carrying capacity modulation via agriculture provides mechanistic link between land use and vector abundance

## Relevance to MalariaSentinel (Centinela)

Demonstrates multi-species modeling approach with agricultural calendar integration relevant to MalariaSentinel's environmental factor incorporation. The agricultural calendar mechanism is directly applicable to Ghana where cocoa farming, seasonal cropping, and irrigation create structured habitat modifications. The multi-species approach (An. gambiae s.l. + An. funestus) aligns with Ghana's vector community composition. The carrying capacity concept connects land use data to vector abundance predictions.

## Limitations

- Madagascar-specific parameterisation; transferability requires calibration
- Agricultural calendar data may be unavailable or coarse in some settings
- Preprint status — peer review pending
- Does not capture insecticide resistance dynamics

## Future Directions

- Adapt agricultural calendar framework for Ghanaian land use patterns
- Validate against Ghanaian entomological survey data
- Integrate with climate change projections for future scenario analysis
- Extend to include larval habitat mapping from remote sensing
