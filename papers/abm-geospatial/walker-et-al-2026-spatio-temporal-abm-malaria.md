# Spatio-temporal agent-based modelling of malaria

**Authors:** James R. Walker, Md Nurul Anwar, et al.
**Journal:** arXiv (preprint) | **Year:** 2026 | **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2505.16240
**File:** papers/abm-geospatial/walker-et-al-2026-spatio-temporal-abm-malaria.md

## Abstract

This paper develops a stochastic spatiotemporal agent-based model of malaria transmission using climatic suitability estimates and household location data. Applied to Vietnam, the model demonstrates that providing some level of protection to a wide range of households reduces malaria prevalence more effectively than providing strong protection to a limited number of households. The work highlights the importance of spatial heterogeneity in intervention targeting strategies.

## Methods

- Stochastic agent-based model with explicit spatial structure at household level
- Climatic suitability estimates used to parameterise local transmission potential
- Household location data for spatially explicit simulation
- Comparison of intervention distribution strategies: broad low-intensity vs. targeted high-intensity
- Sensitivity analysis across spatial and temporal dimensions

## Key Results

- Broad coverage of moderate protection outperforms concentrated high-intensity protection for prevalence reduction
- Spatial heterogeneity in transmission creates non-linear responses to intervention coverage
- Household-level modelling captures fine-scale clustering of transmission
- Stochastic formulation captures realistic variability in outbreak dynamics
- Model validated against surveillance data from Vietnamese provinces

## Relevance to MalariaSentinel (Centinela)

Demonstrates spatial heterogeneity modelling and intervention distribution strategies directly relevant to MalariaSentinel's framework. The finding that broad moderate coverage outperforms targeted intensive coverage has direct implications for the Centinela's resource allocation module — particularly for LLIN distribution and IRS campaigns in Ghana. The household-level spatial resolution aligns with the Centinela's goal of fine-grained risk mapping and targeted response.

## Limitations

- Preprint status — peer review pending
- Limited to Vietnamese epidemiological context
- Climatic suitability as sole environmental driver may underestimate other factors
- Household-level data requirements may be challenging in some settings

## Future Directions

- Extend to multi-species vector dynamics
- Incorporate human behaviour and treatment-seeking patterns
- Validate across multiple endemic settings
- Integrate with real-time surveillance data feeds
