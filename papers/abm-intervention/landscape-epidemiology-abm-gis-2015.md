# Landscape Epidemiology Modeling Using an Agent-Based Model and a Geographic Information System

**Authors:** Various
**Journal:** Land | **Year:** 2015 | **DOI:** 10.3390/land4020378
**File:** papers/abm-intervention/landscape-epidemiology-abm-gis-2015.md

## Abstract

This paper integrates a spatial agent-based model of malaria with a Geographic Information System (GIS) for landscape epidemiology analysis. The framework performs hot spot analysis and ordinary kriging for spatial statistics, demonstrating capabilities for exploring biological insights, monitoring epidemiological landscape changes, and guiding resource allocation across heterogeneous landscapes.

## Methods

- Spatially explicit agent-based model of malaria transmission
- GIS integration for landscape-level analysis
- Hot spot analysis (Getis-Ord Gi*) for identifying transmission clusters
- Ordinary kriging for spatial interpolation of risk surfaces
- Landscape heterogeneity modelling via land use/land cover data
- Scenario analysis for intervention placement optimisation

## Key Results

- Successful integration of ABM with GIS for landscape epidemiology
- Hot spot analysis identifies statistically significant transmission clusters
- Kriging provides continuous risk surface from discrete simulation outputs
- Framework enables exploration of how landscape features affect transmission
- Demonstrated application for resource allocation optimisation

## Relevance to MalariaSentinel (Centinela)

Foundational integration of ABM with GIS for spatial analysis. The ABM-GIS integration approach provides a direct template for the Centinela's architecture: ABM simulation engine + GIS analysis layer. The hot spot analysis and kriging methods are directly applicable to the Centinela's risk mapping module. The landscape epidemiology approach supports the Centinela's goal of understanding how environmental heterogeneity drives transmission in Ghana.

## Limitations

- 2015 publication; GIS and ABM capabilities have advanced significantly
- Limited computational resources may have constrained model complexity
- Does not capture human mobility or treatment-seeking behaviour
- Validation against real-world data not fully described

## Future Directions

- Modernise with current ABM and GIS frameworks
- Add human mobility and treatment-seeking behaviour
- Integrate real-time satellite imagery for dynamic landscape inputs
- Validate against Ghanaian epidemiological and entomological data
