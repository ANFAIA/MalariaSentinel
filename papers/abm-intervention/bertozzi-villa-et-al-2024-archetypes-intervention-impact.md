# An archetypes approach to malaria intervention impact mapping

## Abstract

This study develops a framework for mapping intervention impact using an archetypes approach, providing a systematic method for evaluating intervention effectiveness across different epidemiological and ecological settings. The archetypes categorise transmission environments by their characteristic dynamics, enabling transfer of intervention impact estimates from data-rich to data-poor settings.

## Methods

- Archetypes classification of transmission environments based on epidemiological and ecological characteristics
- Bayesian hierarchical models for intervention impact estimation within each archetype
- Cross-validation of archetype assignments against independent data
- Transferability framework for applying impact estimates across settings
- Sensitivity analysis of archetype definitions and number
- Application to multiple intervention types (ITNs, IRS, treatment, chemoprevention)

## Key Results

- Archetypes approach provides systematic framework for cross-setting comparison
- Distinct archetypes capture meaningful differences in intervention effectiveness
- Transferability framework enables impact estimation in data-poor settings
- Archetype assignments are robust across different classification methods
- Different interventions show different effectiveness profiles across archetypes

## Relevance to MalariaSentinel (Centinela)

Novel approach for intervention impact assessment applicable to MalariaSentinel. The archetypes framework could classify Ghana's districts into transmission archetypes, enabling tailored intervention recommendations per archetype. The transferability approach supports the Centinela's goal of evidence-based intervention selection even where local trial data is limited. The multi-intervention comparison provides a foundation for the Centinela's intervention optimisation module.

## Limitations

- Archetype definitions may oversimplify continuous variation
- Transferability assumes archetype-level generalisability
- Dataset format (Figshare) may limit direct integration
- Does not capture dynamic changes in archetypes over time

## Future Directions

- Apply archetypes framework to Ghana's districts
- Develop Ghana-specific archetype definitions
- Integrate with ABM for mechanistic archetype modelling
- Create operational tool for archetype-based intervention planning
