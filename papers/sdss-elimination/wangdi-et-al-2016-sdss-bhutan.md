# Development and evaluation of a spatial decision support system for malaria elimination in Bhutan

**Authors:** Kinley Wangdi, Cathy Banwell, et al.
**Journal:** Malaria Journal | **Year:** 2016 | **DOI:** 10.1186/s12936-016-1235-4
**File:** papers/sdss-elimination/wangdi-et-al-2016-sdss-bhutan.md

---

## Abstract

This paper describes the development and evaluation of an open-source QGIS-based spatial decision support system for malaria elimination in Bhutan, supporting LLIN distribution planning, IRS targeting, and reactive case detection. The system demonstrated high acceptability among users and successful integration into routine malaria surveillance systems, though challenges in skilled personnel availability and hardware constraints were identified.

## Methods

- Open-source QGIS-based SDSS development
- Modules for: LLIN distribution planning, IRS targeting, reactive case detection
- User acceptability evaluation via structured interviews and usability testing
- Integration assessment with existing routine malaria surveillance systems
- Training programme design and implementation
- Hardware and connectivity requirements assessment

## Key Results

- High user acceptability among malaria programme staff
- Successful integration into routine surveillance workflow
- Improved spatial targeting of LLIN distribution and IRS campaigns
- Reactive case detection module enabled faster response to imported cases
- Key challenges: skilled GIS personnel shortage, hardware constraints, connectivity issues

## Relevance to MalariaSentinel (Centinela)

Practical SDSS implementation model with lessons for MalariaSentinel deployment. The open-source QGIS approach demonstrates a cost-effective technology stack. The user acceptability findings inform the Centinela's user experience design. The identified challenges (skilled personnel, hardware) are directly relevant to the Centinela's deployment strategy in Ghana. The module structure (LLIN, IRS, reactive detection) aligns with the Centinela's planned intervention support components.

## Limitations

- Bhutan-specific context; transferability to Ghana requires adaptation
- QGIS-based desktop application may not support real-time mobile use
- Limited to spatial visualisation; no predictive simulation capability
- Dependent on stable hardware and connectivity infrastructure

## Future Directions

- Extend to web-based platform for mobile access
- Add ABM-based transmission prediction module
- Integrate with mobile health data collection systems
- Develop offline-capable version for remote settings
