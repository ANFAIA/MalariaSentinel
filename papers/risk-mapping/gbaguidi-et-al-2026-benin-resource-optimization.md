# Guiding malaria elimination interventions: a data-driven approach to resource optimization in Benin

**Journal:** Malaria Journal | **Year:** 2026 | **DOI:** 10.1186/s12936-026-06033-5

## Abstract

This study applies high-resolution environmental covariates and stochastic partial differential equation (SPDE) models to map malaria prevalence across Benin, identifying distinct hotspots in northern and central regions. The data-driven approach demonstrates the utility of environmental covariates in capturing spatial variability of malaria transmission and provides an evidence base for resource optimization in elimination planning.

## Methods

- High-resolution environmental covariates: vegetation indices, temperature, rainfall, elevation, land cover
- SPDE-based Bayesian geostatistical model for prevalence mapping
- Model-based clustering to identify transmission hotspots
- Resource optimisation framework linking prevalence maps to intervention allocation
- Cross-validation against Demographic and Health Survey (DHS) data
- Sensitivity analysis of environmental covariate contributions

## Key Results

- Distinct malaria hotspots identified in northern and central Benin
- Environmental covariates capture significant spatial variability in prevalence
- SPDE model provides continuous risk surface with uncertainty quantification
- Northern Benin shows higher prevalence associated with specific environmental conditions
- Data-driven approach enables evidence-based resource allocation for elimination

## Relevance to MalariaSentinel (Centinela)

Practical application of spatial modeling for intervention targeting. The SPDE-based approach provides a validated methodology for the Centinela's prevalence mapping component. The environmental covariate set (vegetation, temperature, rainfall, elevation, land cover) is directly applicable to Ghana. The hotspot identification approach aligns with the Centinela's goal of targeted intervention delivery. The resource optimisation framework provides a template for the Centinela's decision support module.

## Limitations

- Benin-specific context; transferability to Ghana requires recalibration
- Prevalence data from surveys may have limited temporal resolution
- SPDE computational demands may limit real-time updating
- Does not incorporate dynamic transmission modelling

## Future Directions

- Apply SPDE framework to Ghana prevalence mapping
- Integrate with real-time surveillance data for dynamic risk updating
- Couple with ABM for mechanistic transmission prediction
- Develop interactive dashboard for programme managers
