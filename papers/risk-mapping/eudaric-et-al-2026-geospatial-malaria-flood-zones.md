# Geospatial mapping of malaria risk in flood-prone zones of Sub-Saharan Africa

**Authors:** Jeremy Eudaric, Marleen C. de Ruiter, et al.
**Journal:** Scientific Reports | **Year:** 2026 | **DOI:** 10.1038/s41598-026-54811-7
**File:** papers/risk-mapping/eudaric-et-al-2026-geospatial-malaria-flood-zones.md

## Abstract

This study integrates high-resolution flood maps with malaria incidence data across 492 flood-prone zones in 38 Sub-Saharan African countries. Approximately 12 million individuals diagnosed with *Plasmodium falciparum* were found to have been exposed to flooding events. Flood exposure was identified as the main driver of malaria burden in flood zones, with implications for climate-sensitive risk mapping and intervention planning.

## Methods

- Integration of high-resolution global flood maps ( GloFAS-derived) with malaria incidence data
- Analysis across 492 flood-prone zones in 38 Sub-Saharan African countries
- Spatial overlap analysis between flood frequency/intensity and malaria burden
- Statistical modelling of flood-malaria relationships
- Population exposure assessment using high-resolution population grids
- Climate scenario analysis for future flood-malaria projections

## Key Results

- ~12 million Pf-diagnosed individuals exposed to flooding events across the study area
- Flood exposure identified as primary driver of malaria burden in flood zones
- Strong spatial correlation between flood frequency and malaria incidence
- Seasonal flood timing aligns with post-flood malaria peaks
- Climate change projected to increase flood-malaria exposure in many regions

## Relevance to MalariaSentinel (Centinela)

Continental-scale risk mapping approach applicable to MalariaSentinel's geospatial analysis. The flood-malaria relationship is directly relevant for Ghana's Volta Basin and coastal flood-prone areas. The integration methodology (flood maps + incidence data + population grids) provides a template for the Centinela's multi-layer risk mapping. The climate projection component supports long-term planning for Ghana's malaria programme.

## Limitations

- Continental scale may obscure local-level dynamics
- Flood map resolution may miss small-scale flooding events
- Malaria incidence data quality varies across countries
- Does not capture post-flood population displacement effects

## Future Directions

- Apply to Ghana-specific flood zones (Volta Basin, coastal areas)
- Integrate with real-time flood early warning systems
- Add ABM simulation for post-flood transmission dynamics
- Develop operational dashboards for flood-malaria preparedness
